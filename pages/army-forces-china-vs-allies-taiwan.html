Comprehensive Platform-by-Platform Analysis Including Tanks, Artillery, Long-Range Fires, Loitering Munitions, Hypersonic Weapons, and Emerging Systems — Updated June 2026

Executive Summary

The ground battle for Taiwan would be fought across three distinct phases: pre-invasion fires (ballistic and hypersonic missile strikes on Taiwan's defenses), the amphibious-to-beach breaking phase (ZTD-05 assault vehicles storming beaches under direct fire), and the inland combined-arms phase (tanks, mechanized infantry, and artillery fighting through Taiwan's mountainous urban terrain). China's People's Liberation Army Ground Force (PLAGF) and PLA Rocket Force (PLARF) together field the world's largest missile inventory, growing at ~50% in four years to an estimated 3,500 missiles, the most capable amphibious assault vehicle fleet ever assembled for a specific contested-crossing mission, and emerging fourth-generation tank concepts including the Type 100 with an unmanned turret and AI-powered fire control. Taiwan's ground forces, increasingly supplemented by US-supplied M1A2T Abrams tanks, 111 HIMARS launchers with ATACMS, Switchblade and ALTIUS loitering munitions, and indigenously developed Hsiung Feng anti-ship missiles numbering 1,400+ by 2026, are structured to deny, delay, and attrite — not to match China in open maneuver warfare. The US Army's Multi-Domain Task Forces (MDTFs) deployed across the Pacific, wielding Dark Eagle hypersonic weapons, Typhon ground-launched Tomahawk and SM-6 launchers deployed to Japan, and PrSM missiles combat-proven in Operation Epic Fury, constitute the decisive long-range fires layer that would shape the battlefield before any boots touch Taiwan's shores.[1][2][3][4]

PART VI: TAIWAN GROUND FORCES — DEFENSE IN DEPTH

Taiwan's ground doctrine following the 2022–2026 reform cycle is explicitly asymmetric: do not try to out-tank China, out-missile China, or fight a linear conventional battle. Instead, layer defenses to make every kilometer of advance cost-prohibitive.

6.1 Taiwan's Three-Layer Ground Defense Concept

Layer 1 — Pre-Crossing Strike (HIMARS / Hsiung Feng / Mines): - 111 HIMARS + 504 ATACMS: destroy amphibious staging ports, TEL positions, marshaling areas at 16–300 km[32][33] - 1,400+ anti-ship missiles (HF-2/HF-3) deployed on mobile launchers along the coast by 2026[60][4] - Minefield programs across the 13 viable landing beaches

Layer 2 — Beach Breaking Defense: - M60A3 TTS upgraded tanks (250+) and CM-11 Brave Tiger (250+) in prepared firing positions[25][24] - 108 M1A2T Abrams providing a heavy armor reserve in the northern approach corridors[15] - Javelin ATGMs: direct kill of ZTD-05 as it exits the water[20] - Switchblade 300 (720 munitions) and ALTIUS 600M (up to 291 systems) loitering munitions[61]

Layer 3 — Urban and Mountain Defense: - Taiwan's mountainous interior is impassable for armor except via limited road axes — Chinese tanks can only advance along the coastal plains and through mountain passes, creating choke points - Urban warfare training accelerated post-2022; "fortress Taiwan" concept based on Israeli/Ukrainian models - NCSIST "suicide drones" — domestically developed loitering munitions unveiled 2023[62]

Key Observations

The Pre-H-Hour "Missile Campaign" Is China's Greatest Ground-Based Advantage: The PLARF's 3,500-missile arsenal means that in any invasion, the first 30–90 minutes would see hundreds of DF-11s, DF-15s, and DF-16s raining on Taiwan's radar installations, runways, HIMARS positions, Patriot batteries, and command nodes before the amphibious fleet even enters the Strait. Taiwan's ability to survive this first wave — through hardened shelters, dispersal, and mobile HIMARS survivability — is arguably the most important single factor in the ground campaign.[1]

Taiwan's HIMARS Forward Deployment Is a Deterrence Masterstroke: By stationing ATACMS-armed HIMARS on Dongyin Island, 19 km from the mainland, Taiwan forces a binary choice on Beijing: tolerate missiles that can strike Fuzhou Naval Base in 7 minutes and PLARF Base 61 in Huangshan, or launch a pre-emptive strike on Dongyin — which itself constitutes an unambiguous act of war that triggers international response before the amphibious force even launches.[33]

The Type 100 Is the Most Conceptually Disruptive Ground Platform Revealed in 2025: The unmanned turret solves the single most lethal vulnerability in tank warfare — the crew member in the turret. If the AI fire control system, drone-killing APS, and AR crew interface perform as advertised, the Type 100 would be survivable against Javelin, FGM-148, and loitering munition attacks that currently kill even the best Western MBTs in Ukraine. The key uncertainty is whether these systems actually work as claimed.[3][8]

Japan's "Southern Shield" Completes the Allied Ground-Based Fires Architecture: With Type 12 Kai missiles deployed to Kumamoto (range 1,200 km), US Army Typhon deployed to Iwakuni with 1,600 km Tomahawks, and Dark Eagle batteries at 2,775+ km, the entire Taiwan Strait crossing zone is now covered by ground-launched precision fires from the first island chain. The PLA must neutralize these before the amphibious fleet is exposed — which forces China into a broader war with Japan immediately, something Beijing has historically sought to avoid.[44][41][43]
