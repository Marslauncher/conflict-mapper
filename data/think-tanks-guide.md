# The World's Best Geopolitical Think Tanks: A Comprehensive Guide (2026)

## Overview

Geopolitical think tanks shape how governments, militaries, corporations, and civil society understand and respond to global events. From conflict tracking to nuclear policy, great-power competition to climate-security nexus research, these institutions produce the analysis that often defines the terms of the strategic debate. The institutions below are drawn from the University of Pennsylvania's Global Go To Think Tank Index, citation-based rankings, and active output in 2025–2026, and are organized by global reach and regional specialization.

***

## Tier 1: Global Institutions — Broadest Reach & Influence

### 1. Brookings Institution
**Website:** [www.brookings.edu](https://www.brookings.edu)

One of the most cited and influential policy research organizations in the world, Brookings covers foreign policy, economics, governance, and international security. Its Strobe Talbott Center for Security, Strategy, and Technology houses leading scholars on U.S. national security and geopolitics. In October 2025, Brookings hosted its fourth annual Knight Forum on Geopolitics, bringing together Congressional leaders and policy experts to assess the global security environment under the Trump administration. Brookings is particularly known for objective, centrist foreign policy analysis that bridges academic scholarship with policy impact.[^1][^2][^3]

**Known for:** U.S. foreign policy, global governance, international security, democracy research, economic policy, China-U.S. relations

***

### 2. Carnegie Endowment for International Peace
**Website:** [carnegieendowment.org](https://carnegieendowment.org)

Carnegie is a unique global network of policy research centers operating in the United States, Russia, China, Europe, the Middle East, and India. Its mission, more than a century old, is to advance peace through fresh policy ideas and direct engagement with decision-makers. In 2025, Carnegie's researchers published widely read analysis on China's internal security cooperation globally, the risks of international legal norms collapsing, North Korea policy alternatives, and the futility of Trump's Ukraine peace push. Carnegie is consistently ranked among the top five think tanks globally by the University of Pennsylvania's Global Go To Think Tank Report.[^4][^5][^6]

**Known for:** International law, nuclear policy, democracy, Russia-Eurasia, Middle East, China strategy, global order

***

### 3. Council on Foreign Relations (CFR)
**Website:** [cfr.org](https://www.cfr.org)

Based in New York, CFR is perhaps the most prestigious American foreign policy institution, known for its Foreign Affairs journal, its Global Conflict Tracker, and its annual Preventive Priorities Survey. The 2025 PPS — based on polling over 15,000 foreign policy experts — identified China in the South China Sea, the Russia-Ukraine war, and crises in Haiti and South Asia as the highest-priority conflict risks. In 2025, CFR launched a Special Initiative on Securing Ukraine's Future and engaged with current and former White House officials, Ukrainian leaders, and NATO policymakers. CFR's Council of Councils published analysis on BRICS, the G20, global reactions to Trump's foreign policy posture, and more.[^7][^8][^9]

**Known for:** U.S. foreign policy, preventive diplomacy, global conflict tracking, international economics, Foreign Affairs publication

***

### 4. RAND Corporation
**Website:** [rand.org](https://www.rand.org)

Originally established to support the U.S. Air Force, RAND evolved into one of the most methodologically rigorous policy research organizations in the world, funded by the U.S. government as well as private endowments and corporations. RAND's 2026 research examines how artificial intelligence and potential AGI could reshape geopolitics, military operations, and intelligence tradecraft — including a "Day After AGI" simulation series exploring China's deployment of a cyber-AI capability against the United States. RAND is one of the top-ranked think tanks for defense and national security and is known for its probabilistic, evidence-driven approach to long-term strategic problems.[^10][^11][^12]

**Known for:** Defense policy, nuclear strategy, national security, AI and emerging tech, health policy, economic analysis

***

### 5. Eurasia Group
**Website:** [eurasiagroup.net](https://www.eurasiagroup.net)

Eurasia Group bills itself as the world's leading global research and advisory firm, and its annual Top Risks report — authored by founder Ian Bremmer — is one of the most watched geopolitical forecasts in the world. The Top Risks 2026 report identified ten key geopolitical risks including the "US political revolution," China winning the global energy battle, a "Donroe Doctrine" of US interventionism in the Western Hemisphere, and Europe's political fragmentation. Unlike academic think tanks, Eurasia Group operates on a commercial advisory model, providing governments and corporations with tailored geopolitical intelligence.[^13]

**Known for:** Geopolitical risk forecasting, commercial advisory services, annual Top Risks report, political risk analysis for investors

***

### 6. International Crisis Group (ICG)
**Website:** [crisisgroup.org](https://www.crisisgroup.org)

Based in Brussels, ICG is the gold standard for field-based conflict analysis and early warning. Its Watch List publications — updated seasonally — identify the ten countries most at risk of conflict escalation and provide specific policy recommendations to the EU and member states. ICG has active teams embedded in conflict zones from Ukraine to Gaza to the South Caucasus, producing analysis that directly feeds into EU and UN diplomatic efforts. ICG's 2025/2026 workplan focuses on conflict prevention, ceasefire facilitation, and the gender dimensions of armed conflict.[^14][^15][^16]

**Known for:** Field-based conflict analysis, early warning, ceasefire advocacy, UN and EU advisory role, Watch List publications

***

### 7. RAND Corporation (Europe)
See RAND entry above — RAND operates European and international research offices, producing localized security analysis for NATO and European policymakers, separate from its primary Washington base.

***

## Tier 2: World-Class Specialist & Regional Institutions

### 8. International Institute for Strategic Studies (IISS)
**Website:** [iiss.org](https://www.iiss.org)

IISS is the world's leading authority on political-military conflict and is the publisher of *The Military Balance* — the definitive annual reference on the armed forces and defence budgets of more than 170 countries. The 2026 edition of The Military Balance, released in early 2026, assessed Russian force generation in Ukraine, China's military capability advances, and NATO's eastern flank reinforcement. IISS organizes the annual Shangri-La Dialogue in Singapore, which is the premier security summit for the Indo-Pacific region. IISS has offices in London, Washington, Bahrain, Singapore, and Berlin.[^11][^17][^18]

**Known for:** Military Balance publication, Shangri-La Dialogue, armed forces data, defense economics, strategic deterrence, Indo-Pacific security

***

### 9. Chatham House (Royal Institute of International Affairs)
**Website:** [chathamhouse.org](https://www.chathamhouse.org)

One of the world's most respected international affairs institutes, Chatham House is named for the "Chatham House Rule" — the confidentiality norm used in diplomatic and policy circles worldwide. In 2025, Chatham House hosted Ukrainian President Zelenskyy, Syrian Foreign Minister Al-Shaibani, and Iraqi Prime Minister Al-Sudani, demonstrating its convening power at the highest levels of global politics. Its researchers provided evidence to UK Parliamentary committees on topics from European security to nuclear proliferation, and the institute played a role in UN resolutions on Haiti and the Global Plastics Treaty. Chatham House's 2026 programmes cover Africa, the Americas, Asia-Pacific, Europe, the Middle East, Russia and Eurasia, and global governance.[^19][^20][^21]

**Known for:** Global policy research, Chatham House Rule, Europe security, Russia-Eurasia, Middle East, UK foreign policy advising

***

### 10. Stockholm International Peace Research Institute (SIPRI)
**Website:** [sipri.org](https://www.sipri.org)

SIPRI, based in Sweden, is the world's premier independent research institute on conflict, armaments, arms control, and disarmament. Its flagship *SIPRI Yearbook* — published annually by Oxford University Press — is the definitive compendium on world military expenditure, international arms transfers, and nuclear arsenals. The SIPRI Yearbook 2025 warned that a "dangerous new nuclear arms race is emerging" as arms control regimes weaken, identifying rapid AI advances, cyber capabilities, and space assets as new destabilizing factors in nuclear deterrence calculations. SIPRI data on military spending and arms transfers is cited in virtually every serious strategic analysis worldwide.[^22][^23]

**Known for:** SIPRI Yearbook, nuclear arsenals data, arms transfers, military expenditure, disarmament research, conflict tracking

***

### 11. Belfer Center for Science and International Affairs (Harvard Kennedy School)
**Website:** [belfercenter.org](https://www.belfercenter.org)

The Belfer Center is Harvard's premier national security research institution and home to Graham Allison's *Avoiding Great Power War Project*, which tracks U.S.-China-Russia dynamics through the framework of Thucydides's Trap. In 2025, Belfer hosted more than 60 events on nuclear security and strategic stability alone, released a Task Force Report on Transatlantic Defense at the Munich Security Conference, and launched the Critical and Emerging Technologies Index. The center's journal *International Security* was ranked #1 in Impact Factor by Clarivate in 2025, underscoring its scholarly stature.[^24]

**Known for:** Great power war prevention, nuclear security, intelligence studies, transatlantic defense, Harvard Kennedy School policy pipeline

***

### 12. Atlantic Council
**Website:** [atlanticcouncil.org](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org)

The Atlantic Council is the foremost U.S. institution focused on transatlantic security and NATO affairs. Its Scowcroft Center for Strategy and Security shaped the terms of debate at the 2025 NATO Summit in The Hague, where allies made an unprecedented commitment to raise defense spending to 5 percent of GDP by 2035. The Council's Global Foresight 2025 survey — drawing on expert forecasters worldwide — projected a world increasingly divided into China-aligned and U.S.-aligned blocs, with nearly half of respondents seeing Russia, Iran, China, and North Korea as formal allies by 2035. The Atlantic Council also established itself as the leading authority on global trade disruption following Trump's tariff agenda.[^25][^26][^27]

**Known for:** NATO and transatlantic security, global foresight, U.S.-Europe relations, democracy promotion, energy security, trade policy

***

### 13. Royal United Services Institute (RUSI)
**Website:** [rusi.org](https://www.rusi.org)

Founded in 1831, RUSI is the world's oldest defense and security think tank. Based in London, RUSI covers military doctrine, defense procurement, counterterrorism, cyber security, nuclear proliferation, and financial crime. It has been declared an "undesirable organization" in Russia — a testament to the quality and impact of its Russia-Ukraine analysis. RUSI produces rapid-response analysis on emerging security developments and is a key advisory body to the UK government, MoD, and NATO partners.[^28][^29]

**Known for:** UK defense policy, military doctrine, nuclear proliferation, counter-terrorism, financial crime, cyber security, Ukraine conflict analysis

***

### 14. Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS)
**Website:** [csis.org](https://www.csis.org)

CSIS is a bipartisan Washington-based think tank focused on defining the future of national security. It was recognized as the Defense and National Security Center of Excellence in the University of Pennsylvania's Global Go To Think Tank Report for three consecutive years. CSIS has dedicated programs on Europe and Russia, Asia, the Middle East, and emerging technologies. It is particularly influential in shaping U.S. policy on China, the Indo-Pacific, and defense modernization, and its scholars frequently testify before Congress and advise senior executive branch officials.[^30][^10]

**Known for:** U.S. national security, China and Indo-Pacific strategy, Europe-Russia, cybersecurity, defense modernization, bipartisan policy research

***

### 15. Institute for the Study of War (ISW)
**Website:** [understandingwar.org](https://www.understandingwar.org)

ISW has become the most widely cited daily source of open-source intelligence on the Russia-Ukraine conflict. Since Russia's full-scale invasion in February 2022, ISW has published a daily *Russian Offensive Campaign Assessment* providing running intelligence estimates of Russian military objectives, operations, and battlefield outcomes. ISW's maps and operational assessments are cited by governments, military analysts, and journalists worldwide. ISW has assessed, for example, that Russian forces captured 4,831 square kilometers in Ukraine during 2025 while suffering approximately 416,570 casualties.[^31][^32]

**Known for:** Russia-Ukraine war tracking, open-source battlefield intelligence, daily operational assessments, defense and security analysis

***

### 16. German Institute for International and Security Affairs (SWP)
**Website:** [swp-berlin.org](https://www.swp-berlin.org)

SWP advises the German Bundestag, the German Federal Government, and international organizations including the EU, NATO, and the UN. It is widely considered Germany's most influential foreign policy research institution and is listed among the world's top 30 think tanks in the University of Pennsylvania rankings. SWP was specifically highlighted in the "100 Think Tanks to Watch 2025" report for its work on volatile great-power behavior, neurotechnology and foreign policy, and supporting civil society actors in shrinking civic spaces.[^33][^34][^1]

**Known for:** German and European foreign policy, EU-NATO advisory role, great-power dynamics, security policy, arms control

***

### 17. Lowy Institute for International Policy
**Website:** [lowyinstitute.org](https://www.lowyinstitute.org)

Australia's leading international policy think tank, the Lowy Institute combines independent nonpartisan research with a global and Asia-Pacific focus. The Lowy Institute Poll — conducted annually — is the most authoritative survey of Australian public opinion on international affairs. In 2025, the institute's research identified three key trends reshaping the Indo-Pacific: accelerating climate-defense nexus impacts, deepening conflict and geostrategic competition, and the blurring of civilian-military domains through technology. The institute publishes *The Interpreter*, a daily foreign policy analysis outlet.[^35][^36][^1]

**Known for:** Indo-Pacific security, Australia-Asia foreign policy, annual Lowy Institute Poll, climate-security nexus, regional multilateralism

***

### 18. Mercator Institute for China Studies (MERICS)
**Website:** [merics.org](https://www.merics.org)

MERICS, based in Berlin, is the largest European research institute focused exclusively on contemporary China. It is also the leading provider of China intelligence to EU decision-makers, with an office in Brussels in addition to its Berlin headquarters. MERICS runs the annual European China Talent Program, the China Tech Observatory, and produces regular policy briefings on China's political economy, technology strategy, and security policy. MERICS has faced direct pushback from Beijing — including sanctions — because of the independence and critical quality of its research.[^30]

**Known for:** China policy analysis, EU-China relations, technology and industrial policy, Chinese governance, European China expertise

***

### 19. Observer Research Foundation (ORF)
**Website:** [orfonline.org](https://www.orfonline.org)

India's most internationally prominent think tank, ORF is based in New Delhi and operates programs covering international relations, security, economics, energy, and technology. ORF's President Samir Saran moderates the Raisina Dialogue — India's premier annual geopolitics conference, hosted in partnership with the Ministry of External Affairs. In 2025, ORF analysts published widely read research on India's strategic handling of flashpoints in Myanmar, Bangladesh, and Pakistan; PLARF threats; India-EU relations; and the geopolitics of BRICS. ORF reflects India's growing influence as a major voice in Indo-Pacific and Global South affairs.[^37][^38]

**Known for:** India foreign policy, Indo-Pacific strategy, Raisina Dialogue, Global South, energy and climate policy, India-EU relations

***

### 20. French Institute of International Relations (IFRI)
**Website:** [ifri.org](https://www.ifri.org)

Founded in 1979, IFRI is France's premier international think tank and one of Europe's most respected. It publishes *Politique étrangère*, France's oldest international affairs journal, and the annual *Ramses* strategic atlas, alongside more than 150 open-access publications per year. IFRI is notable for its Observatory for Future Conflicts — a structured forecasting program monitoring geopolitical, security, and socio-economic dynamics out to 2040, with programs covering East-Central Africa and Turkey 2050. IFRI ranked in the top five globally in the University of Pennsylvania Think Tank rankings.[^39][^4][^1]

**Known for:** French and European foreign policy, long-range strategic forecasting, Politique étrangère journal, transatlantic relations, global governance

***

### 21. European Council on Foreign Relations (ECFR)
**Website:** [ecfr.eu](https://www.ecfr.eu)

Founded in 2007, ECFR is a pan-European think tank with offices in seven European capitals: Berlin, Paris, Madrid, London, Rome, Warsaw, and Sofia. ECFR is distinguished by its council-based model — bringing together former heads of state, ministers, and senior policymakers from across the EU as members. The institute's four core research programs cover Asia and China, Wider Europe, European Power, and the Middle East and North Africa. ECFR is particularly known for its "European Sovereignty" agenda, pushing EU member states toward more unified and autonomous foreign and security policy.[^39]

**Known for:** European foreign policy, EU sovereignty research, transatlantic relations, China-EU dynamics, pan-European policy network

***

### 22. German Council on Foreign Relations (DGAP)
**Website:** [dgap.org](https://www.dgap.org)

DGAP (Deutsche Gesellschaft für Auswärtige Politik) is a Berlin-based think tank providing foreign policy analysis and strategic recommendations to German and European policymakers. In early 2025, DGAP produced a major memo series titled "Global Germany" following the German federal election, identifying priorities for the Merz government — including European security leadership, managing the US-China competition, and economic sovereignty. DGAP is closely networked with the German Foreign Office and Bundestag and is influential in shaping Germany's evolving strategic posture.[^40]

**Known for:** German foreign policy, EU strategy, transatlantic relations, energy security, German security culture, European power

***

### 23. Clingendael — Netherlands Institute of International Relations
**Website:** [clingendael.org](https://www.clingendael.org)

Clingendael is the Netherlands' premier international relations research and training institute. It combines original policy research with one of Europe's leading diplomatic training academies — the Clingendael Academy — which trains diplomats, civil servants, and security professionals from dozens of countries each year. The institute focuses on European security, fragile states, peacebuilding, and the dynamics of Dutch and EU foreign policy.[^33]

**Known for:** European security, diplomatic training, fragile state analysis, EU foreign policy, peacebuilding

***

### 24. Peterson Institute for International Economics (PIIE)
**Website:** [piie.com](https://www.piie.com)

PIIE is the world's leading institution for international economic policy research and was named the International Economics Center of Excellence in the University of Pennsylvania rankings. While not exclusively geopolitical, PIIE's work on trade, sanctions, geoeconomics, and the weaponization of finance is indispensable in today's geopolitically contested economic environment. PIIE scholars have been at the forefront of analyzing the economic fallout from U.S.-China decoupling, Russian sanctions regimes, and the Trump tariff agenda.[^10]

**Known for:** International trade policy, sanctions and geoeconomics, global financial architecture, currency policy, WTO reform

## Key Considerations When Using Think Tank Analysis

Think tanks range from genuinely independent research institutions to organizations with close ties to governments, political parties, or industries. A few important distinctions to keep in mind:

- **Independence vs. state-linked:** SIPRI, ICG, and Chatham House are widely considered genuinely independent. CICIR (China) and RIAC (Russia) are state-linked and should be read with this context in mind.[^44][^45]
- **Ideological orientation:** The Heritage Foundation and Hoover Institution lean conservative; Brookings and Carnegie are generally centrist; ECFR and Bruegel are broadly Europeanist and multilateralist in orientation.[^45][^42]
- **U.S.-centric bias:** RAND, CSIS, and CFR produce excellent research but, as U.S.-funded institutions, rarely cross core American strategic interests in their findings.[^45]
- **Commercial vs. academic:** Eurasia Group operates on a subscription/advisory model rather than as a public research institution, which shapes its outputs toward near-term risk forecasting rather than long-form scholarship.[^13]
- **Emerging market voices:** ORF (India), the Lowy Institute (Australia), and ISS (South Africa) offer non-Western perspectives increasingly important for understanding the Global South's strategic calculus.[^35][^37]
